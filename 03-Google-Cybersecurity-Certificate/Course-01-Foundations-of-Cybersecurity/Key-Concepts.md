# What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting:

- Networks
- Systems
- Devices
- Data
- People

from unauthorized access, cyber attacks, and other security threats.

---

# The CIA Triad

The foundation of information security consists of three core principles:

- **Confidentiality** – Prevent unauthorized access to information.
- **Integrity** – Ensure data remains accurate and unaltered.
- **Availability** – Ensure systems and data are accessible when needed.

---

# Why Cybersecurity Matters

Cybersecurity helps organizations:

- Protect sensitive information.
- Maintain business operations.
- Meet legal and regulatory requirements.
- Protect customer privacy.
- Preserve trust and reputation.

---

# Threat Actors

A **Threat Actor** is any individual or group capable of causing security risks.

Threat actors generally fall into two categories:

- **External Threats** – Hackers, cybercriminals, nation-state groups.
- **Internal Threats** – Employees, contractors, vendors, or partners.

# Protecting Personal Information

### Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

Information that can identify an individual, such as:

- Full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Home address

### Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)

Highly sensitive personal information, including:

- Financial information
- Medical records
- Biometric data

---

# Identity Theft

Identity theft is the unauthorized use of another person's personal information for fraudulent purposes, often involving financial gain.

---

# Security Tools

### SIEM

Collects and analyzes security logs from multiple sources to detect potential threats.

### IDS

Monitors network or system activity and alerts security teams about suspicious behavior.

### Python

Used to automate repetitive cybersecurity tasks and data analysis.

### SQL

Used to query, retrieve, and analyze data stored in databases.
