# 📖 Glossary
> Google Cybersecurity Certificate - Course 1: Foundations of Cybersecurity

This glossary contains key cybersecurity terms learned throughout Module 1. It will continue to grow as I progress through the Google Cybersecurity Certificate.

---

## A

### Availability
Ensuring that systems, services, and data are accessible to authorized users whenever needed.

---

## C

### CIA Triad
A security model based on three core principles:
- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

### Cloud Security
The practice of protecting cloud-based systems, applications, and data.

### Compliance
Following laws, regulations, standards, and organizational security policies.

### Confidentiality
Protecting information from unauthorized access or disclosure.

### Cybersecurity
The practice of protecting networks, systems, devices, and data from cyber threats.

---

## D

### Data Breach
An incident where sensitive information is accessed, disclosed, or stolen without authorization.

### Digital Forensics
The process of collecting, preserving, and analyzing digital evidence during security investigations.

---

## E

### Ethical Hacking
Authorized security testing performed to identify vulnerabilities before attackers can exploit them.

---

## I

### Identity Theft
The illegal use of another person's personal information for fraud or financial gain.

### Incident Response
The process of detecting, investigating, containing, and recovering from cybersecurity incidents.

### Integrity
Ensuring that data remains accurate and unaltered.

### Intrusion Detection System (IDS)
A system that monitors network or system activity for suspicious behavior.

---

## N

### Network Security
Protecting computer networks from unauthorized access and cyber attacks.

---

## P

### Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
Information that can identify an individual, such as a name, email address, or phone number.

### Phishing
A social engineering attack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information.

### Python
A programming language widely used to automate cybersecurity tasks.

---

## S

### Security Control
A safeguard designed to reduce security risks and protect organizational assets.

### Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)
A platform that collects, analyzes, and monitors security logs from multiple sources.

### Security Posture
An organization's overall ability to prevent, detect, and respond to cyber threats.

### Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (SPII)
Highly sensitive personal information, such as financial, medical, or biometric data.

### SQL
A language used to query and manage databases.

---

## T

### Threat Actor
An individual or group that poses a cybersecurity threat.

### Threat Landscape
The overall environment of current cyber threats, attack methods, and vulnerabilities.

---

- New terms will be added as I complete additional modules.
