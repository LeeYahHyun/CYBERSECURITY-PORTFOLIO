## 📚 Module 1 - Foundations of Cybersecurity

### 1. What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, devices, data, and people from unauthorized access and cyber threats.

---

### 2. The CIA Triad

- **Confidentiality**: Protecting information from unauthorized access
- **Integrity**: Ensuring data is not altered or tampered with
- **Availability**: Ensuring systems and data are accessible when needed

---

### 3. Why Cybersecurity Matters

- Protect organizational data
- Maintain business continuity
- Safeguard users
- Preserve company reputation

---

### 4. Threat Actors

Threat actors are individuals or groups that pose security risks:

- External: hackers, cybercriminals
- Internal: employees, contractors, partners

---

### 5. Common Entry-Level Roles

- Security Analyst: Monitor and respond to security incidents
- Security Engineer: Build and maintain security systems
- SOC Analyst: Monitor alerts and investigate threats

---

### 6. Required Skills

**Technical Skills:**

- IDS systems

---

### 7. Sensitive Data

- **PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**: Email, phone number, address
- **SPII (Sensitive PII)**: Financial, medical, biometric data

---

### 8. Identity Theft

The misuse of someone’s personal information for fraud or impersonation.
